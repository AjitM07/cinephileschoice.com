Kill Bill - Vol 1

Director: Quentin Tarantino

Release Year: 2003

Genre: Action

Story

A pregnant assassin, code-named The Bride, goes into a coma for four years after her ex-boss Bill brutally attacks her. When she wakes up, she sets out to seek revenge on him and his associates.

Review

Kill Bill is one hell of a thrill. The bright colour palette does a wonderful job at highlighting the over-the-top nature of the film. Kill Bill is admittedly little more than a stylish revenge thriller -- albeit one that benefits from a wildly inventive surfeit of style. "Kill Bill, Volume 1" shows Quentin Tarantino so effortlessly and brilliantly in command of his technique that he reminds me of a virtuoso violinist racing. Brutally bloody and thrillingly callous from first to last, Kill Bill covers its action in a kind of delirium-glaze.
