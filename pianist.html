The Pianist

Director: Roman Polanski

Release Year: 2002

Genre: Biopic

Story

During the WWII, acclaimed Polish musician Wladyslaw faces various struggles as he loses contact with his family. As the situation worsens, he hides in the ruins of Warsaw in order to survive.

Review

The Pianist is a moving, heartbreaking, and ultimately uplifting story that stays with you long after the credits roll. Its a terrific movie, if relentlessly gritty and realistic. Powerful true story of Jewish pianist has brutal violence. It was overwhelming because of the maliciousness portrayed by the Nazi soldiers towards the Polish and Jewish. Overall, The Pianist is one of the most detailed and shocking accounts of the treatment of the Jews by the Nazis.
