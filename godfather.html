The Godfather

Director: Francis Ford Coppola

Release Year: 1972

Genre: Drama

Story

Don Vito Corleone, head of a mafia family, decides to hand over his empire to his youngest son, Michael. However, his decision unintentionally puts the lives of his loved ones in grave danger.

Review

THE GODFATHER is quite simply a masterful piece of film-making, an epic in the truest sense of the word and by far the finest gangster film ever shot. The story by Mario Puzo and Francis Ford Coppola is a brilliant conjuring act, inviting us to consider the Mafia entirely on its own terms.The Godfather is a long, minutely detailed movie of some three hours, there naturally isn't time to go into the backgrounds and identities of such characters.
