Gone Girl

Director: David Fincher

Release Year: 2014

Genre: Thriller

Story

Nick Dunne discovers that the entire media focus has shifted on him when his wife, Amy Dunne, mysteriously disappears on the day of their fifth wedding anniversary.

Review

"Gone Girl" is a meticulously crafted psychological thriller that expertly deconstructs marriage, media influence and public perception. A brilliant example of a book-to-film adaptation that managed to stay faithful to the novel, while also injecting the story with its own inventive style. "Gone Girl" is art and entertainment, a thriller and an issue, and an eerily assured audience picture. It is also a film that shifts emphasis and perspective.
