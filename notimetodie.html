No Time To Die

Director: Cary Joji Fukunaga

Release Year:

Genre:

Story

After leaving the active services, James Bond leads a peaceful life in Jamaica. However, a call for help from an old friend triggers a confrontation with a treacherous enemy.

Review

'No Time to Die' honorably concludes the Daniel Craig chapter of the Bond epic. While it's overall a bumpy ride; “No Time to Die” is definitely a great Bond film, and the perfect swansong for Daniel Craig. A look that is both opulent and generic; a tone that mixes brisk professionalism with maundering self-pity. This film is visually stunning, and in parts quite dazzling.
